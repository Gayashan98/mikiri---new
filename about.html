The Legend of purity

Our Authentic Heritage

Sustaining generations of traditional dairy expertise, R.K. Dhanesh brings the raw, untouched purity of Sri Lankan village curd to modern homes.

Generations of Craftsmanship

Crafted by Tradition, Refined by Purity

The R.K. Dhanesh legacy began with a simple belief: dairy products should remain untouched, authentic, and naturally healthy. For decades, our family has worked closely with organic local buffalo breeders to extract high-quality, high-fat milk.

Today, we continue that legacy. Every single *Kiri Hattiya* (clay pot) is hand-poured and fermented in brick-walled humidor cellars under precise family secrets. We do not use commercial machinery or instant setting enzymes. The thick, creamy top-layer cream is a natural biological occurrence of slow firewood boiling, a hallmark of real Hambantota curd.

"Curd making is not a manufacturing line; it is an art of patience. The clay pot must breathe, the woodfire must glow, and the milk must settle in its own beautiful time."

The Source of Creaminess

Pasture Sourced Buffalo Herd

Superior curd can only be made from superior milk. Our water buffaloes graze freely on organic grass pastures rich in mineral nutrients under the bright Hambantota sun.

Unlike cow's milk, buffalo milk has twice the fat, high calcium, and highly digestible biological structures, yielding curd that is naturally firm, incredibly thick, and low in lactose. Our dairy herds are loved, cleaned daily, and fed organically, ensuring they produce the sweetest, most nutritional milk in the region.

Quality Pillars

Our Purity Standards

🥛

Zero Water Dilution

Most commercial curds are mixed with water, powdered milk, or starch to increase volume. At R.K. Dhanesh, we enforce a strict 100% whole milk standard. We boil the milk slowly until it reduces in volume naturally, intensifying the proteins.

🏺

Authentic Porous Clay Pots

Plastic containers seal in water vapor, leading to a watery curd. Our authentic clay pots are baked locally by traditional potters. The natural micro-pores in the clay allow the curd to "sweat", draining excess moisture out while cooling, yielding a robust, firm consistency.

🦠

Natural Culture Fermentation

Instead of artificial laboratory powders, we propagate our curd using traditional "starter curd" (*kiri mudun*) from previous premium batches. This natural microflora yields a complex probiotic spectrum with a delicate, balanced lactic sourness.
